# CS230
Gaming Room Client Software Design Doc
-Briefly summarize The Gaming Room client and their software requirements. Who was the client? What type of software did they want you to design?
  The Gaming Room is a client that wanted to take an existing browser app, and extend their reach to a new audience.  The goal was to understand the requirements 
  that would accompany each of the possible choices of systems to adapt.
  
-What did you do particularly well in developing this documentation?
  I think I did a good job of analyzing and discerning between different systems for the application.

-What about the process of working through a design document did you find helpful when developing the code?
  This process will definetly help me organize my thoughts in the future. One problem I often run into while coding is knowing
  all the parts i need to include, but not necessarily where they belong in the program, or how they are defined. Usually i learn 
  this through cutting my teeth on the program and reoganizing the components as i go.

-If you could choose one part of your work on these documents to revise, what would you pick? How would you improve it?
  I would probably redo the requirements section as i felt my decisions closely matched other areas of the document.

-How did you interpret the user’s needs and implement them into your software design? Why is it so important to consider the user’s needs when designing?
  I think it is fair to say that when helping someone with their project it is easy to think you know what the customer wants, however easy to deliver a product
  that is what you want at it's core. Using documents like this can be great in recording the precise needs and to keep the design process in order.

-How did you approach designing software? What techniques or strategies would you use in the future to analyze and design a similar software application?
  UML documentation at least on paper will be a focus of my future designs. Understanding what i will need to implement is easy to conceptualize
  but sometimes had to implement. Creating pen and paper UML docs will allow me to flesh out my ideas prior to making a mess in an IDE that can 
  then be difficult to debug later on.
